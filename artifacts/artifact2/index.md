## Key Differences

| Machine Learning | Deep Learning |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Requires feature engineering | Learns features automatically |
| Works well with smaller datasets | Performs best with large datasets |
| Faster to train | Computationally intensive |
| Easier to interpret | Less interpretable |
| Uses traditional algorithms | Uses neural networks |

## Reflection

This assignment strengthened my understanding of how Machine Learning and Deep Learning solve different categories of problems.

One of my biggest takeaways was realizing that Deep Learning is not always the best solution. Machine Learning remains highly effective when datasets are smaller, interpretability is important, or computational resources are limited.

Researching real-world applications also helped me understand why organizations choose different AI approaches depending on their business needs.

Completing this artifact improved both my technical understanding and my ability to communicate AI concepts clearly.
